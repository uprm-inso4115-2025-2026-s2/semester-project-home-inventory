== 1.3.1 Scope and Span


Scope

The project’s scope is to develop a student-focused digital home inventory system that helps students living independently (alone or with roommates) track household items, reduce waste from expired products, avoid duplicate purchases, and improve budgeting. The system will address common pain points caused by relying on memory and informal tracking, and it will support both individual and shared-household workflows. The project will encompass several key areas:

•⁠  ⁠Domain Engineering: Analyze how students currently manage household items, including common breakdowns such as forgetting what is available, inconsistent updates, and poor roommate coordination. Identify the major actors (solo students, roommates, budget-conscious students) and the domain concepts (items, categories, rooms, quantities, thresholds, expiration dates, shared ownership).

•⁠  ⁠Requirements Engineering: Define system requirements that support inventory accuracy, fast item updates, and coordination. Requirements will include adding/updating/removing items, organizing inventory by room and category, low-stock and expiration tracking, search/filtering, shared inventory visibility, and budgeting/spending tracking. Requirements will also address constraints such as busy schedules, low tolerance for complex setup, and the need for clear privacy controls.

•⁠  ⁠Software Architecture: The architecture will feature a Flutter mobile front-end and a Supabase back-end (PostgreSQL + Auth + Storage). The system will support user authentication, persistent inventory data, and synchronization for shared households. Notification logic will support low-stock and expiration reminders with configurable thresholds. The overall system will be designed using Clean Architecture principles to maintain separation of concerns and testability.

•⁠  ⁠Software Design: The design will prioritize a quick, low-friction workflow for maintaining inventory. This includes simple item creation/edit flows, consistent category and room organization, shared vs. personal item indicators, and clear visual feedback for low-stock and expiring items. Budget and report views will present spending and inventory summaries in a clear, student-friendly way.

•⁠  ⁠Software Process: The project will follow an Agile, feature-team structure. Each feature team owns its feature end-to-end (research, design collaboration, implementation, testing, and documentation). Work will be tracked through GitHub issues and reviewed via pull requests into team branches and then into ⁠ main ⁠. Continuous feedback from team walkthroughs and scenario reviews will guide refinement of requirements and UX decisions.

Span

The project’s span focuses on a scalable and user-friendly solution that supports typical student households and can expand over time. The initial span is targeted toward students managing a modest number of household items and coordinating with a small number of roommates. The system is designed to support efficient inventory maintenance, timely reminders, and budget visibility without requiring excessive user effort.

•⁠  ⁠Specifics of the System: Users can maintain a centralized inventory organized by room and category, update item quantities, and optionally track expiration dates and priorities. Users can configure notification thresholds for low-stock and upcoming expirations. Shared households can view a common inventory and use ownership indicators (shared vs. personal) to reduce duplicates and conflict. Budget tracking connects purchases to categories and provides basic spending summaries and reports.

•⁠  ⁠Target Audience and Expansion: The initial span targets students living independently, including those living with roommates. Expansion may include richer analytics (usage rates and trends), more advanced report types, optional integrations with external productivity or budgeting tools, and improved automation for restock planning. Any expansion will remain centered on student needs and low-friction maintenance.

•⁠  ⁠Methodology and Maintenance: The project will iterate through repeated cycles of documentation, implementation, validation, and testing. Effectiveness will be tracked through milestone success criteria (correct inventory updates, accurate notifications, fast and accurate filtering, correct spending calculations, and reliable shared syncing). The cycle of feedback and refinement will ensure the system remains practical for real student workflows and constraints.
