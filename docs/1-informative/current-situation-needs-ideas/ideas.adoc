== Ideas

Outline candidate solution directions. Ideas are not commitments; they are options to address the needs. The idea below focuses on solution directions and capabilities; implementation technology choices are deferred to later sections.

=== Lightweight Smart Inventory

* Core concept: A lightweight household inventory tool optimized for very fast item capture and shared visibility in student households, so records stay current with minimal effort.
* Needs addressed: N1, N2, N3, N4, N5
* Key capabilities:
  - Fast item capture with minimal information required at the moment of entry
  - Shared household view of what items are available and who last updated them
  - Quick search and filtering to check item availability
  - Optional prompts to review items that may be running low or expiring (kept low-noise)
  - Works during temporary connectivity gaps and reconciles changes when connectivity returns

* Expected benefits:
  - Reduces the hassle of item entry
  - Improves long-term inventory accuracy
  - Prevents duplicate purchases
  - Enables smooth roommate coordination
  - Reduces the chance that people disable reminders by keeping prompts minimal and relevant

* Risks / unknowns:
  - Users may still abandon after initial novelty wears off
  - Conflicting updates when multiple household members change the same item list around the same time
  - Temporary connectivity gaps may lead to mismatched inventories until updates reconcile
  - User adoption of shared features may vary

* How we will validate this idea:
  - Run quick usability sessions to see whether people can capture an item fast enough during realistic moments (e.g., after shopping)
  - Track weekly retention during pilot testing
  - Test with small shared households (2-4 users)
  - Observe whether optional prompts are perceived as helpful or disruptive (and whether people disable them)
