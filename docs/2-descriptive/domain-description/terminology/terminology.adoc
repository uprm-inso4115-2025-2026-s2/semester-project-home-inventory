== Terminology

This section defines the domain’s ubiquitous language: preferred terms for entities, actions, events, and rules observed in student households. The definitions aim to stay independent of any specific system-to-be or implementation.

=== Domain Terms

Terms that emerged from domain analysis (interviews, observations, and informal household practices).
These terms are defined independently of any specific system-to-be or implementation technology.

[cols="2,1,4,3", options="header"]
|===
| Term
| Type
| Definition
| Synonyms / Notes

| Household
| Entity
| A living unit (dorm, apartment, shared house) where one or more people manage household essentials.
| Previously "Home."

| Household member
| Entity
| A person who belongs to a household and participates in consuming, replenishing, or coordinating around items.
| Previously "Owner" or "Occupant."

| Location
| Entity
| A storage place within a household (kitchen cabinet, pantry area, bathroom closet, car trunk, hallway bin).
| Previously "Room."

| Item type
| Entity
| The kind of an item, independent of a specific package (e.g., "Milk", "Dish soap", "Toilet paper").
| Previously "Product."

| Item instance (package)
| Entity
| A concrete package or unit of an item type that physically exists in the household (e.g., a specific milk carton).
| Previously "Stock."

| Inventory (reality)
| Entity
| The set of item instances that physically exist in the household at a given time.
| Real-world state, not a list or record.

| Tracked inventory record
| Artifact
| A representation of availability used by household members (fridge note, list, memory, chat message). May diverge from reality.
| Supports the "tracked vs. reality" distinction.

| Restock list / planner
| Artifact
| A household-oriented list of items that should be replenished soon (fridge note, notes-app list, chat).
| Delivery method is not part of the domain.

| Alert / notification (domain-level)
| Artifact / Event
| A message that informs a household member about an actionable condition (low-stock, out-of-stock, expiring, expired, budget threshold crossed).
| Delivery method is an implementation choice.

| Spending entry
| Record
| A record of a financial transaction in a household (purchase, refund, or adjustment), optionally linked to an item type and category.
| Supports budgeting and reporting.

| Transaction (quantity change)
| Record
| A record of an availability change (consume, restock, or adjust) with a time and reason.
| Supports traceability and verification.

| Availability
| Property
| A household's current understanding of how much of an item is left, often qualitative ("full", "almost empty", "backup").
| May later be represented as quantity.

| Quantity
| Property
| A more precise measure of availability (count or units). Can be unknown or approximate; must never be negative.
| —

| Expiration date
| Property
| A date after which an item instance is considered expired or undesirable. Applies to some items, not all; may be unreadable or missing.
| —

| Low-stock threshold
| Rule / Property
| A boundary value used to decide when an item is considered low.
| May be a fixed quantity initially.

| Budget cycle (period)
| Concept
| The time window over which spending is compared to a budget (weekly, monthly, custom). Resets and rollovers are policy decisions.
| —

| Backup supply
| Practice / Concept
| A household practice of keeping a spare instance or package to reduce the risk of running out.
| Often used instead of numeric tracking.

| Spending category
| Entity / Label
| A grouping for spending (e.g., groceries, cleaning, personal care). May align with item categories, but is not required to.
| —

| Budget
| Entity / Rule
| A financial limit defined for a period (household-level or category-level) used to evaluate spending.
| Budget window and cycle are requirements-level decisions.

| Consumption
| Action
| Using an item over time, reducing its availability. Rate may vary by context (exam weeks, guests).
| —

| Replenishment (restock)
| Action
| Acquiring more of an item, increasing availability. Often triggered after low-stock, out-of-stock, or expiration events.
| —

| Snooze / defer
| Action
| A household member delays acting on an alert for a period of time.
| Domain intent: "remind me later."

| Dismiss / ignore
| Action
| A household member acknowledges an alert and chooses not to act on it, at least for now.
| Should not cause repeated alerts without a meaningful state change.

| Low-stock judgment
| Event / Rule
| A decision that an item is running low, based on a household strategy ("feels light", "opened backup") or a numeric threshold.
| Not inherently numeric.

| Out-of-stock
| State / Event
| The condition or event where an item is considered unavailable because it is gone (quantity at zero, or judged empty).
| Distinct from low-stock.

| Near-expiry
| State / Event
| An item instance approaching its expiration date within a defined window (e.g., N days).
| Window definition belongs in requirements.

| Expired
| State / Event
| An item instance past its expiration date.
| Expired does not always mean unavailable; policy varies by household.

| Budget threshold crossing
| Event
| The moment when spending reaches or exceeds a defined threshold (e.g., 80%, 100% of the budget).
| Can trigger alerts, subject to anti-spam rules.

| Item acquired
| Event
| A household member obtains an item instance (possibly in bulk) and brings it into the household.
| "Bought 24 yogurts from Costco."

| Item stored
| Event
| An item instance is placed in a household location.
| "Rice stored in kitchen cabinet; backup in hallway bin."

| Item consumed
| Event
| A household member uses an item, decreasing its availability.
| Consumption rate may increase during exams or when guests visit.

| Backup opened
| Event
| A household member begins using the backup supply of an item.
| Often used as a qualitative low-stock signal.

| Replenishment decided
| Event
| A household member decides the household should acquire more of an item type.
| May be prompted by low-stock, out-of-stock, or expiration.

| Item replenished
| Event
| New item instances of an item type are acquired to restore availability.
| Can be duplicated if coordination fails.

| Duplicate purchase
| Event
| More of an item type is acquired unnecessarily because household members acted on inconsistent information.
| Common for milk, eggs, and rice per interviews.

| Item approaching expiration
| Event
| An item instance is within a near-expiry window.
| Motivates timely use or discard decisions.

| Item expired
| Event
| An item instance passes its expiration date (if it has one).
| Policy on what to do varies by item type and household.

| Item discarded
| Event
| A household member throws away an item instance (expired, spoiled, or unwanted).
| Can occur even when another unopened instance exists.

| Alert generated
| Event
| A low-stock, out-of-stock, near-expiry, expired, or budget condition becomes actionable and is communicated to household members.
| Delivery method is not part of the domain event.

| Alert dismissed / deferred
| Event
| A household member acknowledges an alert and chooses to ignore it or act later.
| Supports snooze and anti-spam behavior.

| Restock entry created / updated
| Event
| An item is added to or updated on the restock list due to low-stock, out-of-stock, or expired conditions.
| May be manual or derived from judgments.

| Spending recorded
| Event
| A household member logs a spending transaction (purchase, refund, or adjustment).
| May be shared or personal.

| Budget cycle started / ended
| Event
| A new budget period begins or a period closes.
| Supports reset and rollover policies.

| Household membership changed
| Event
| A member moves in or out; household participation changes.
| Motivates access removal expectations later.

|===

=== Requirements Terms

Terms introduced when specifying the capabilities of the system-to-be.
These terms refine or operationalize domain concepts but remain independent of UI layout and implementation technology.

[cols="2,1,4,3", options="header"]
|===
| Term
| Type
| Definition
| Synonyms / Notes

| User
| Actor
| A person who interacts with the system-to-be through its interface. Corresponds to a household member in the domain.
| "User" is the system-facing term; "Household member" is the domain-facing term.

| Household admin
| Role
| The single household member with administrative responsibility at a given time. Transferable before the admin leaves (DR-4).
| Domain equivalent: informal "household manager" role.

| Membership state
| Property
| The status of a user's participation in a household (active, pending, revoked).
| No direct domain artifact.

| Reorder point (threshold)
| Rule / Property
| A quantity value at or below which an item type is considered low. Initial requirements may accept a fixed per-item threshold.
| Refines the domain's "low-stock threshold" with a numeric constraint.

| Near-expiry window
| Rule / Property
| The definition of "expiring soon" — the number of days before expiration that triggers a near-expiry alert.
| Window value is a requirements decision deferred from the domain.

| Deduplication / anti-spam rule
| Rule
| A constraint preventing repeated alerts or repeated restock-list additions when no meaningful state change has occurred.
| Operationalizes the domain's "should not cause repeated alerts."

| Actionable state
| Concept
| A system-recognized condition (low-stock, out-of-stock, near-expiry, expired, budget-threshold-crossed) that may trigger an alert or restock entry.
| Refines domain-level events into system-detectable states.

| Budget status (derived)
| Concept
| A computed view of spending versus budget within a cycle: amount spent, amount remaining, percentage used, and threshold flags.
| Derived from spending entries; not directly observable in the domain.

| Scheduled sweep
| Function
| A periodic background check that evaluates low-stock and expiration conditions without requiring the user to open the system.
| Automation mechanism; delivery details are implementation-level.

| Notification record
| Record
| A persisted representation of an alert, including type, timestamp, and read/unread status, available at minimum for in-app review.
| Refines the domain-level "alert"; adds persistence and status tracking.

|===

=== Implementation Terms

Terms introduced during architectural and technical design.
These terms are platform- or technology-specific and do not appear in domain or requirements documents.

[cols="2,1,4,3", options="header"]
|===
| Term
| Type
| Definition
| Synonyms / Notes

| Auth (authentication)
| Component
| The subsystem handling user registration, login, session management, password reset, and identity for authorization checks (Supabase Auth / JWT).
| —

| Session
| Concept
| A JWT-based authenticated context for a user interacting with the system.
| —

| Row Level Security (RLS)
| Mechanism
| A database-layer policy (Supabase/Postgres) ensuring users can only access data belonging to their household(s).
| —

| PostgREST / API layer
| Component
| The middleware that exposes database tables and views as REST endpoints and enforces database constraints (Supabase PostgREST).
| —

| Supabase Realtime
| Component
| The mechanism by which the mobile app subscribes to live household data changes (e.g., shared inventory updates) without manual refresh.
| —

| Dashboard
| UI Surface
| A screen in the Flutter mobile app that presents key inventory and spending metrics at a glance (IR-08).
| —

| Push notification
| Delivery Mechanism
| A device-level notification delivered by the platform push provider; one possible delivery method for domain-level alerts.
| Contrast with in-app notification list.

| In-app notification list
| UI Surface
| A screen or panel within the app listing persisted notification records; the other possible delivery method for domain-level alerts.
| Contrast with push notification.

|===
