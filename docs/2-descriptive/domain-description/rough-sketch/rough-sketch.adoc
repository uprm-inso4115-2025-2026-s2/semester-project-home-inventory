=== Interview Snippets and Raw Observations

==== Shared Apartment - 3 Students (Interview, Jan. 20, 2026)

* *#Student 1#:* "If it is not on our fridge note, we assume we are out."
* *#Student 2#:* "Sometimes I check the pantry before leaving, but by the time I get to Walmart I forgot what we needed."
* *#Student 3#:* "We have rice in two places. One bag in the kitchen cabinet and another in a plastic bin in the hallway."
* #Current tracking method observed: fridge sticky note + WhatsApp group chat.#
* #Observed responsibility pattern: no one described being formally responsible for keeping track of quantities.#
* #Shared example reported: milk is sometimes bought twice in the same week.#
* #When asked how they know something is low, participants answered with phrases such as "when it feels light" and "when we open the backup."#
* #The backup opening is not usually written down.#

==== #Student 4 - Living Alone# (Interview, Feb. 2, 2026)

* *#Student 4#:* "I buy in bulk from Costco when I go with my parents."
* *#Student 4#:* "I got like 24 yogurts once."
* *#Student 4#:* "Some of them expired."
* #Follow-up note: participant estimated throwing away several yogurts during the last semester.#
* *#Student 4#:* "I keep cleaning supplies in the bathroom closet and under the kitchen sink."
* *#Student 4#:* "I don't count exactly. I just look."
* *#Student 4#:* "I use the Notes app sometimes, but then I stop."
* *#Student 4#:* "If it takes more than a few seconds to log, I won't do it."

==== Shared Household - 2 Adults, 1 Child

* #One household member reported inviting family members to a shared grocery list app.#
* #One adult reportedly does most purchases.#
* #Reported situation: a child may use the last unit of an item without telling the others.#
* #Reported example: toilet paper runs out unexpectedly when guests visit.#
* #Medicines are kept in a separate drawer because expiration matters more there.#
* #Reported situation: expired medicine was discarded without realizing there was another unopened box.#
* #Reported expectation: access should be removable when someone moves out.#

==== #Student 5 - Student Athlete# (Interview, Feb. 2, 2026)

* *#Student 5#:* "During exam weeks I eat way more snacks."
* *#Student 5#:* "Protein bars last me like two weeks normally, but during midterms maybe four or five days."
* *#Student 5#:* "I don't really adjust it formally. I just buy more when they're gone."
* *#Student 5#:* "I just remember how fast it ran out last time."

==== Observations from Informal Brainstorming Session

* #Participants often describe availability with words like "full", "almost empty", or "backup", and less often with exact numbers.#
* #Items repeatedly mentioned as essentials included toilet paper, soap, toothpaste, milk, and rice.#
* #Perishables repeatedly mentioned included yogurt, vegetables, chicken, and medicine.#
* #Bulk buying was usually justified by lower price per unit.#
* #Waste was mentioned more often for food than for cleaning supplies.#
* #Duplicate buying was mentioned most often for milk, eggs, and rice.#
* #Some participants organize by location such as kitchen, bathroom, or laundry area; others do not.#
* #No participant described doing a periodic full inventory check.#
* #Multiple participants said they have forgotten what they already had at home.#
* #Some participants said they prefer taking a pantry picture instead of writing a list.#

==== Edge Situations Mentioned

* Barcode does not scan because packaging is damaged.
* Expiration date is unreadable or missing.
* Guests consume items faster than usual.
* Someone moves out and still has access to the shared list.
* Backup is stored in a car trunk and forgotten.
