== Events

Domain events represent instantaneous state changes in the domain that matter to stakeholders. Each event is phrased in the past tense to emphasize that a state change has just occurred. Events are independent of any particular system-to-be, even if a later system represents or reacts to them.

[cols=”2,4,4”,options=”header”]
|===
|Event |Description |Notes / examples

|Item instance acquired
|A household member has just obtained an item instance and it has become available in the household.
|”24 yogurts have just arrived from Costco.” Two related state changes: the obtaining and the becoming-available-in-household may be distinct events in practice.

|Item instance stored
|An item instance has just been placed in a household location.
|”Rice has just been stored in kitchen cabinet; backup placed in hallway bin.”

|Item instance consumed
|A household member has just used an item, and its availability has decreased.
|Consumption rate may change during exams/guests.

|Backup opened
|A household member has just begun using the backup supply of an item.
|Often used as a qualitative low-stock signal.

|Low-stock judged
|An item has just been judged as running low.
|”Feels light” / “backup opened” / threshold crossed.

|#Out-of-stock reached#
|#An item's availability has just reached zero.#
|#”Toilet paper has just run out unexpectedly.”#

|Replenishment decided
|A household member has just decided the household should acquire more of an item type.
|May be prompted by low-stock, out-of-stock, or expiration.

|Item instance replenished
|#New item instance# of an item type have just been acquired, restoring availability.
|Can be duplicated if coordination fails.

|Duplicate purchase occurred
|More of an item type has just been acquired unnecessarily because members acted on inconsistent information.
|Common for milk/eggs/rice per rough sketch.

|#Item instance reached near-expiry#
|#An item instance has just entered its near-expiry window.#
|Motivates timely use or discard decisions.

|#Item instance expired#
|#An item instance has just passed its expiration date.#
|Policy varies by item type/household.

|#Item instance discarded#
|#A household member has just thrown away an item instance.#
|Can occur even when another unopened instance exists.

|Alert generated
|A low/out/near-expiry/expired/budget condition has just become actionable and has been communicated to household members.
|Delivery method is not part of the domain event.

|Alert dismissed / deferred
|A household member has just acknowledged an alert and chosen to ignore it or act later.
|Supports “snooze” and anti-spam behavior.

|Restock entry created/updated
|An item has just been added to the restock list/planner due to low/out/expired conditions.
|May be manual or derived from judgments.

|Spending recorded
|A household member has just logged a spending transaction.
|May be shared or personal.

|Budget threshold crossed
|Spending in a budget cycle has just reached a warning level or exceeded the limit.
|May trigger an alert.

|Budget cycle started/ended
|A new budget period has just begun, or a period has just closed.
|Supports reset/rollover policies.

|Household membership changed
|A member has just moved in or out; household participation has changed.
|Motivates access removal expectations later.
|===
