= 2.1.5 Events, Actions, and Behaviors
Author: Jorge L. De Leon Orama


This section presents the main events, actions, and behaviors observed in the environment of a household inventory. It clearly defines the events, actions, and behaviors that are relevant to the domain of Home Inventory.

* Events: The instantaneous occurrence that marks the beginning of a new state in the system.
* Actions: Individual acts performed by a user or system that contribute to changing the state.
* Behaviors: Processes consisting of various small actions that together lead to an event.

== Incorporation of Goods into Household Inventory

=== Event 
* The inventory now contains recorded household items.

=== Actions
* Introduction of goods into the household inventory.

=== Behavior
* The process of acquiring goods, which enter the household and become part of the inventory.


== Maintenance of Household Inventory

=== Event
* The inventory now reflects updated information.

=== Actions
* Modification of inventory contents.

=== Behavior
* The process of keeping track of items in the inventory to maintain accurate records of household goods.

== Increment of Household Inventory
=== Event 
*  The recorded quantity of an item has increased.

=== Action
* Addition of an already existing item to the inventory.

=== Behavior
* The process of replenishment of goods to maintain availability over time. 

== Consumption and Depletion of Household Inventory

=== Event
* The recorded quantity of an item has decreased.

=== Action
* Removal of an already existing item from the inventory.

=== Behavior
* The process of consumption or usage, reducing item quantity over time.

== Detection of Inventory Conditions and Needs

=== Event
* An item has reached a low-stock condition.

=== Action
* Recognition of diminished availability of an item.

=== Behavior
* The process of supply evaluation as items deplete over time.

== Stock Management of Household Inventory

=== Event
* An item is now considered sufficiently stocked.

=== Action
* Recognition of adequate availability of an item.

=== Behavior
* The process of maintaining a satisfactory item quantity level over time.

== Item Availability

=== Event
* An item is now no longer available.

=== Action
* Exhaustion of an item's quantity.

=== Behavior
* The process of item quantity depletion over time.

== Item Expiration

=== Event
* An item is now expired.

=== Action
* Recognition of an item becoming expired.

=== Behavior
* The natural process of an item's lifecycle as time-sensitive goods move beyond their usable time period.


== Restock Detection

=== Event 
* A need for replenishment has been identified.

=== Action 
* Recognition of an item's quantity decreasing below a defined threshold.

=== Behavior
* The process of adjusting household supplies as quantities decrease and are restored over time. 

== Grocery/Restock List

=== Event
* A grocery or restock list now exists.

=== Action
* Compilation of items that require replenishing.

=== Behavior
* The process of preparing for the replenishment of household supplies as needs arise over time.

== Linked Household Inventory

=== Event
* The inventory is now shared between multiple people.

=== Action
* Establishment of shared access to the inventory.

=== Behavior
* The process of coordinating inventory information among multiple participants as shared resources are collectively maintained over time.
