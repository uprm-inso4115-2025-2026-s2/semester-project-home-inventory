= Budget Analyzer Concept Formation

== Objective

Apply domain analysis to the budget and spending model in a home inventory system.
The goal is to define clear spending concepts, group expenses into higher-level categories, identify inconsistencies in financial data, and rewrite the data in a structured way for analysis.

== Solution

=== 1. Identify and Abstract Key Concepts

* *Expenses*: item-related spending such as purchases, repairs, insurance, storage, and maintenance
* *Spending Patterns*: recurring spending vs. one-time spending
* *Budget Tracking*: tracking spending trends and detecting unusual or conflicting records

=== 2. Categorize Concepts

* *Fixed Expenses*: regular, predictable costs such as insurance, storage fees, and maintenance plans
* *Variable Expenses*: irregular or one-time costs such as buying furniture, replacing electronics, or unexpected repairs

=== 3. Identify and Resolve Potential Inconsistencies

* *Conflicting recurring amounts*: the same monthly cost appears with different values in different records
** Resolution: keep one current value and flag older or conflicting entries for review

* *Wrong categorization*: a yearly premium is recorded as monthly, or a one-time purchase is recorded as recurring
** Resolution: reclassify recurring costs by billing period and one-time costs as variable expenses

* *Duplicate purchase records*: the same item purchase appears more than once
** Resolution: merge duplicate entries and keep one verified record

* *Missing item links*: an expense exists without being tied to an item or item category
** Resolution: assign the expense to the correct item or mark it as uncategorized until reviewed

* *Value conflicts*: purchase price, current value, and replacement cost do not align
** Resolution: treat purchase price as historical cost, replacement cost as future estimate, and current value as present estimate

=== 4. Rewrite Financial Data into a Structured Narrative

The budget analyzer manages financial data linked to household items.

Fixed expenses are recurring costs such as insurance, storage, and maintenance plans.
These are tracked over time as regular entries.

Variable expenses are one-time or irregular costs such as item purchases, replacements, and repairs.
These are tracked as separate spending events.

Spending behavior is analyzed by comparing recurring costs, one-time purchases, and value-related data such as purchase price and replacement cost.
The system improves clarity by checking for duplicate records, conflicting amounts, incorrect categories, and missing item links.

== Risks

* some expenses may be hard to classify as fixed or variable
* users may enter incomplete or conflicting data
* duplicate entries may come from manual and imported records
