== Domain Literature Review 
Summaries of key texts to justify system logic, specifically addressing the challenges of manual data entry and the synchronization burden.

=== Personal Information Management (PIM) 
* **Core Finding:** Users struggle when items are physical but tracking is digital. The mental effort to bridge this gap in real-time is the primary cause of system abandonment.
* **Application to Project:** To minimize the synchronization burden for busy students, the design must move away from real-time micro-updates.
** *Periodic Reconciliation:* Implement a Weekly Inventory Snapshot. Instead of tracking every glass of milk, the app prompts a batch update to align digital records with physical reality.
** *Event-Driven Triggers:* By leveraging location services, the app can trigger a notification when the user is at a grocery store: "You're at Selectos. Do you need to move 'Milk' from 'Empty' to 'Full'?" This shifts the burden from memory to environmental cues.

=== Household Food Waste Patterns 
* **Core Finding:** Students fail to track inventory because they lack situational visibility. They don't know what they have until they open the cupboard.
* **Application to Project:** The app must act as an external memory. By providing a digital mirror of the cupboard, it reduces double-buying caused by memory lapses during high-stress academic periods.

=== The Endowment Effect in Asset Management
* **Core Finding:** Owners overvalue items they manually track.
* **Application to Project:** Manual entry, while a friction point, serves as a Commitment Device. By interacting with the item's cost during entry, students become more conscious of waste, supporting the project goal of budget management.

== Research Note Cards
Discrete facts formatted for the domain model.

**RN-001: Manual Entry Friction & Abandonment**

* **Category:** Stakeholder Behavior
* **Fact:** In manual data entry systems, every additional field reduces user retention.
* **Implication for App:** Support minimum viable entry. A user should be able to log an item with just a name and status in under three seconds to prevent abandonment.


**RN-002: Subjective Categorization**

* **Category:** Intrinsic
* **Fact:** Without standardized barcodes, categorization is subjective, like "Batteries" in "Electronics" vs. "Utility".
* **Implication for App:** Use a "Multi-Tagging" system rather than a rigid folder hierarchy, allowing items to exist in multiple logical locations.


**RN-003: Prospective Memory & Cues**

* **Category:** Cognitive Psychology
* **Fact:** Task execution is higher when linked to an environmental cue as Location/Time, rather than abstract intention.
* **Implication for App:** Replace the "always-on" tracking requirement with specific "Triggered Sessions" to capture data when the user is already in a "management" mindset.


== Identified Inconsistencies & Abstractions

=== State-Based Tracking
* **Observation:** Tracking specific volumes in a manual system is a "High-Friction" task that students will ignore.
* **Recommendation:** Use broad **States** instead of integers for consumables.
** *Target UX:* Status: Full -> Open -> Low -> Empty.

=== Inconsistency
* **Observation:** The project requests "Automated notifications when an item is low," but a manual system has no real-time visibility.
* **Refined Requirement:** Move from Quantity-Based alerts to Probability-Based or Event-Based alerts.
** *Example:* "It has been 6 days since you bought Bread; it is likely Low or Expired. Please verify."
