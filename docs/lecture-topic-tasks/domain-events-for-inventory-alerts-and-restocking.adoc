== Objective

Identify and document *domain events* for the Alerts & Restock slice of the Home Inventory system. A domain event is something meaningful that *already happened* in the domain (past tense, business-significant), which other parts of the system may react to—for example by creating alerts, updating read models, or suggesting grocery items. The notion aligns with event-oriented modeling in domain-driven design <<evans-ddd>>.

This document satisfies the issue success criteria (<<issue-322>>): a named list of events, a short *when* and *why* for each, and an explicit link to alerts, expiration monitoring, or restocking behavior.

== Bounded context

These events are phrased around *household inventory* as the core aggregate (products, stock lines, quantities, expiration dates) and the *alert/restock* concerns that depend on that data. They are conceptual: the current Flutter code mostly *evaluates* state in use cases (<<src-expiration>>, <<src-auto-exp>>, <<src-stock>>, <<src-auto-stock>>) rather than publishing a formal event bus. Treating these as domain events still clarifies what *could* be raised when rules fire or when users change data.

== Domain events

The following tables group events by theme. Names are suggestive (PascalCase); teams may align them with implementation types or message schemas later.

=== Expiration monitoring

[options="header"]
|===
| Domain event | When it occurs | Why it matters (typical reactions)

| `NearExpirationDetected`
| A stock line’s expiration date falls within the configured “near” window (e.g. within 7 days before expiration, per default in <<src-expiration>>), evaluated against “today.”
| Drive *approaching expiration* alerts, badge counts, and prioritization in the UI; may combine with user alert intervals in backend automation (<<alert-states>>).

| `ExpirationReached`
| The expiration date is today or in the past (`ExpirationStatus.expired` in domain logic; <<src-expiration>>).
| Drive *expired* alerts, discourage consumption prompts, or trigger cleanup workflows.

| `ExpirationDateRenewed`
| The user (or authorized process) updates a stock line’s expiration date to a future date after it was near or past expiration.
| Reset expiration-related alert state (aligns with *Renewed* in <<alert-states>>); suppress stale notifications.

| `ExpirationTrackingCleared`
| Expiration date removed or item/stock line removed from inventory.
| Stop expiration checks and cancel pending expiration alerts for that line.
|===

=== Stock level and restocking

[options="header"]
|===
| Domain event | When it occurs | Why it matters (typical reactions)

| `LowStockThresholdCrossed`
| Reported quantity moves from above the low-stock threshold to at or below it (`StockStatus.lowStock` in <<src-stock>>).
| Drive *low stock* alerts and dashboard highlights; prompt planning or grocery list additions.

| `StockDepleted`
| Quantity becomes zero (`StockStatus.outOfStock`; <<src-stock>>).
| Drive *out of stock* alerts; stronger urgency than low stock; may link to shopping or restock tasks.

| `StockReplenished`
| Quantity increases (purchase, delivery recorded, or correction)—especially from zero or from a low band back to comfortable levels.
| Clear or downgrade stock alerts; update restock suggestions; optional “restocked” confirmation for the household.

| `StockThresholdConfigurationChanged`
| Household or product-level threshold rules change (e.g. default “5 units” in <<src-stock>> overridden per item).
| Recalculate who is low/out without a quantity change; avoid false positives/negatives in alerts.
|===

=== Alert and preference lifecycle (supporting)

These sit at the boundary between *domain* and *application* but are included because <<issue-322>> ties events to alert generation.

[options="header"]
|===
| Domain event | When it occurs | Why it matters (typical reactions)

| `AlertDispatchRequested`
| Policy decides a human should be notified (after deduplication, snooze, and channel rules).
| Create notification records, push payloads, or in-app inbox entries (<<alert-seq>>).

| `AlertSnoozedOrDismissed`
| User defers or clears a notification; preferences updated.
| Suppress repeat alerts until the next interval or state change (`last_triggered`, `is_snoozed` in <<alert-states>>).

| `AlertChannelPreferenceChanged`
| User enables/disables push, changes device token, or switches in-app-only behavior.
| Route future `AlertDispatchRequested` outcomes correctly.
|===

== Mapping to current application code

Useful anchors under `src/lib/features/alerts_restock/`: <<src-expiration>> classifies a single line into `ExpirationStatus`; <<src-auto-exp>> scans inventory for near-expired and expired lines; <<src-stock>> classifies quantity into `StockStatus`; <<src-auto-stock>> scans for low and out-of-stock lines.

Core inventory persistence and updates live under <<src-core-inv>>; a *quantity or date change* there is the usual *cause* of `StockReplenished`, `LowStockThresholdCrossed`, `ExpirationDateRenewed`, etc., even if those are not emitted as objects today.

== Downstream reactions (not always separate domain events)

Reactions often belong to other bounded contexts:

* *Grocery list* — may subscribe to `LowStockThresholdCrossed`, `StockDepleted`, or `NearExpirationDetected` to suggest items (the grocery feature today is largely UI/local state; integration is a future concern).
* *Reports and insights* — may aggregate counts of alerts raised or items restocked over time.
* *Scheduled automation* — edge functions or cron jobs may periodically re-evaluate inventory and emit or persist alert-related outcomes, as sketched in <<alert-seq>>.

== Boundaries and overlap (issue risks)

* *Other teams* own full notification transport, Supabase schemas, and parts of inventory CRUD. This list describes *what happened in the domain*, not which service implements it.
* *Evaluation vs. discrete event*: Automated checks can run on a schedule; the same physical item might satisfy “near expiration” on multiple days. Product decisions define whether each run raises a new `NearExpirationDetected` or only on first entry to the window (deduplication / `last_triggered_alert` in <<alert-states>>).
