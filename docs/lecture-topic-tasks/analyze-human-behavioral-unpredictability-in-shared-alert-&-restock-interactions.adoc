= Lecture Topic Task: Analyze Human Behavioral Unpredictability in Shared Alert and Restock Interactions

== Objective

Analyze how *inconsistent or unpredictable* household-member behavior affects the *reliability* of the Alert & Restock slice in *shared student housing*: where several people share one tracked inventory, informal coordination already fails in ways the domain narrative calls *coordination failures* <<unified-narrative>>. The lecture lens combines *human behavior facets* (cognitive and social tendencies that shape how people use systems) <<facets-human>>, *biddable behavior* (people may not honor stated expectations or interface prompts even when the machine “asks” correctly), and *unreliable actors* (members who update late, differently, or not at all) relative to interface and domain requirements such as IR-02 <<stakeholder-req>>.

This document satisfies the issue success criteria (<<issue-554>>): unpredictable behaviors are named and illustrated; effects on alerts, restock automation, and inventory accuracy are explained; mitigation strategies are included; and lecture concepts are tied to Alert & Restock flows and existing documentation.

== Bounded context

The focus is *multi-member households* in a student-housing setting <<narrative>>, the *tracked inventory record* that may diverge from physical reality <<entities>>, and Team 2 concerns: *alerts* (low/out/near-expiry/expired), *restock or grocery automation* driven from inventory-derived flags <<grocery-restock>>, and policies such as deduplication and snooze <<alert-states>> <<alert-seq>>. The system’s domain rules (e.g. stock and expiration classification <<src-stock>> <<src-expiration>>) are largely *deterministic* given their inputs; *unpredictability* enters through *who* updates data, *when*, and *how* members respond to notifications—so reliability is a joint property of software and social practice.

== Unpredictable behaviors and system effects

The following tables group behaviors by theme. They are not an exhaustive psychology catalog; they connect recurring patterns to Alert & Restock outcomes and align with broader human-behavior analysis <<facets-human>> and human-error scenarios <<model-human-error>>.

=== Alert and notification responses

[options="header"]
|===
| Unpredictable behavior | Typical manifestation (shared housing) | Effects on alerts, restock automation, and inventory accuracy

| Ignoring or batching attention to alerts
| Push or in-app alerts dismissed unread; “inbox zero” never; only one roommate reads notifications.
| Alert *delivery* succeeds but *action* does not; restock lists stay stale; same underlying stock state may re-trigger alerts unless deduplication and state transitions are carefully defined <<alert-states>> <<alert-seq>>.

| Chronic snooze or defer without state change
| User silences reminders while physical stock continues to drop or age.
| Reduces noise short term but can mask true urgency; automation may still add restock lines from flags while humans disagree on who acts <<grocery-restock>>.

| Heterogeneous trust in automation
| Some members follow system lists; others shop from memory or a parallel group chat.
| *Duplicate purchasing* or *missed purchasing* relative to the tracked record; inventory numbers diverge further after unlogged consumption or unlogged bulk buys <<unified-narrative>>.

|===

=== Inventory hygiene and logging

[options="header"]
|===
| Unpredictable behavior | Typical manifestation (shared housing) | Effects on alerts, restock automation, and inventory accuracy

| Forgetting to update quantities after use
| Snacks, toiletries, or shared milk consumed without a log entry; “someone will update it.”
| Quantities stay high in the system while reality is low—*false negatives* for low-stock and out-of-stock alerts; restock automation may not fire when it should <<src-stock>> <<grocery-restock>>.

| Consuming without logging (present bias, friction)
| Immediate convenience of not opening the app wins over logging <<facets-human>>.
| Same as above; expiration-only signals may still fire if dates exist, but *quantity-based* reliability collapses.

| Incorrect or duplicate data entry
| Wrong quantity, wrong product, or duplicate line after a manual shop.
| *False positives* for low/out alerts; spurious restock suggestions; wasted notifications and list clutter <<model-human-error>>.

| Delayed entry after a real restock
| Groceries put away first; app updated hours or days later.
| Temporary mismatch: alerts may still show low/out after the household already restocked; members lose trust in alert timing <<alert-seq>>.

|===

=== Coordination and shared responsibility

[options="header"]
|===
| Unpredictable behavior | Typical manifestation (shared housing) | Effects on alerts, restock automation, and inventory accuracy

| Social loafing on shared tasks
| Everyone assumes someone else will log or shop <<facets-human>>.
| Tracked record drifts; alert and automation policies optimized for a single diligent user misfire under diffuse responsibility.

| Unclear ownership (personal vs shared)
| Item treated as communal in reality but marked personal (or vice versa) <<model-human-error>>.
| Wrong person alerted or wrong restock priority; disputes and duplicated “fixes” to the same line.

| Parallel channels (chat vs app)
| Decisions happen in group chat; the app is not updated to match <<narrative>>.
| System-derived alerts reflect *artifact* state, not *agreed* roommate state—classic coordination failure <<unified-narrative>>.

|===

== Mitigation strategies and examples

These strategies do not assume perfect users; they reduce harm from *biddable* and *unreliable* participation <<facets-human>> <<issue-554>>.

* *Lower logging friction* — quick adjust quantity, barcode or template flows, optional “used one” gestures so consumption events match reality more often (addresses false negatives for <<src-stock>>-driven alerts).
* *Attribution and visibility* — show who last changed a line or acknowledged an alert so social loafing is harder to hide <<facets-human>>; aligns with IR-style expectations that members can see shared state <<stakeholder-req>>.
* *Targeted routing* — notify the member who usually consumes an item type, or rotate a lightweight “inventory champion” role, instead of broadcasting identical alerts to everyone.
* *Honest automation* — restock auto-add rules <<grocery-restock>> should tolerate stale flags (e.g. confirm-before-push, merge duplicates, or require recent confirmation) so list spam does not train users to ignore the app.
* *Alert policy tuning* — deduplication, snooze caps, and state-based suppression <<alert-states>> <<alert-seq>> balance annoyance vs. signal when behavior is irregular.
* *Channel alignment* — optional lightweight “we bought this” or chat deep-link so parallel coordination leaves a trace in the tracked record <<unified-narrative>>.
* *Ownership clarity* — explicit personal vs. shared markers reduce wrong accountability and duplicate buys <<entities>> <<model-human-error>>.

== Mapping to Alert & Restock design and code

Team 2’s stock and expiration logic is *pure over current model data*: `checkStock` and `checkExpiration` classify lines from stored quantity and dates <<src-stock>> <<src-expiration>>; `automatedCheckStock` and `automatedCheckExpiration` aggregate results over the inventory <<src-auto-stock>> <<src-auto-exp>>. There is no built-in model of *which member* is reliable or whether a quantity was just consumed in the kitchen; *human unpredictability* therefore shows up as *bad or stale inputs* and *uneven follow-through* on outputs. Restock automation requirements explicitly consume flags such as `isLowStock`, `isOutOfStock`, and `isExpired` <<grocery-restock>>—all of which inherit whatever inaccuracy roommates introduce. Notification design (deduplication, `last_triggered`, snooze) <<alert-states>> is partly a response to *known* human attention limits but cannot fully correct missing logs.

== Downstream consequences (not only “bugs”)

Consequences spill across bounded contexts:

* *Budget and spending* — duplicate buys and emergency runs show up as unplanned cost; not strictly Team 2 but linked to failed restock coordination <<narrative>>.
* *Reports* — dashboards summarize the *record*, not the pantry; misleading trends if logging is uneven.
* *Member trust* — if alerts are often “wrong” from the user’s perspective because data is stale, users disable channels or ignore future alerts <<facets-human>>.

== Boundaries and overlap (issue risks)

* *Variation across households and cultures* — mitigations must be configurable; one policy will not fit all living arrangements <<issue-554>>.
* *Overlap* with <<facets-human>>, <<model-human-error>>, and coordination narratives <<unified-narrative>>; this lecture task narrows the lens to *Alert & Restock reliability* rather than replacing those documents.
* *Milestone scope* — some mitigations (passive sensing, hardware, org-wide policies) may exceed current implementation; they are still valid *design directions*.
