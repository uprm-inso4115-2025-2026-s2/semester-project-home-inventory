== #Unified Narrative#

=== #1. The Core Cycle#

#A *Household* is a living unit managed by one or more *Household Members*. Members
continuously engage in a cycle of acquiring, storing, and consuming *Household Items*.
Items are kept in one or more *Locations* within the household, and each item carries a
set of *Item Attributes* — including quantity, optional expiration date, purchase date,
and price — that together define its current state.#

#As consumption occurs, the *Availability State* of a *Household Item* decreases. When
availability crosses a recognized threshold — whether judged numerically (quantity drops
below a set limit) or qualitatively ("the bottle feels light," "we just opened the last
backup") — the item is considered *Low-Stock*. If supply is fully depleted the item is
*Out-of-Stock*. If an expiration date passes before consumption, the item is *Expired*.#

=== #2. The Tracking Gap#

#A *Household* never has perfect knowledge of its own *Inventory*. What members actually
know is not the inventory itself, but a *Tracked Inventory Record* — an approximation
assembled from informal artifacts such as mental notes, shared chat messages, whiteboard
lists, and recalled receipts. This record diverges from reality whenever a *Household Item*
is consumed, moved, opened, or acquired without the record being updated.#

#The divergence between the *Tracked Inventory Record* and the real *Inventory* is the
primary source of *Coordination Failure*: situations where one or more *Household Members*
act on stale or incomplete information and produce outcomes such as duplicate purchases,
missed restocks, or undetected expiration.#

=== #3. Replenishment#

#When a *Household Member* judges an item to be *Low-Stock* or *Out-of-Stock*, a
*Replenishment Action* is triggered. This action may be planned (added to a restock list
for the next shopping trip) or immediate (purchased on the same day). A *Replenishment
Action* results in new *Household Items* entering a *Location*, and the corresponding
entry in the *Tracked Inventory Record* is updated to reflect the new *Availability State*.#

#A *Replenishment Action* can fail or be deferred — for instance, if budget constraints
prevent immediate purchase, or if the responsible *Household Member* is unaware that the
action is needed due to a stale *Tracked Inventory Record*.#

=== #4. Expenditure and Budget#

#Every *Replenishment Action* incurs an *Expenditure*: a recorded amount paid, attributed
to a payer, scoped as either personal or shared, and optionally linked to a specific
*Household Item* or spending category. In shared *Households*, the scope of an
*Expenditure* (personal vs. shared) is a frequent source of dispute.#

#A *Budget* constrains how much a *Household* or a *Household Member* may spend within a
given cycle (e.g., monthly). When cumulative *Expenditures* within the cycle approach or
exceed the *Budget*, a threshold crossing is recorded and surfaced for review. Budget
awareness directly influences *Replenishment Actions* — members may delay restocking,
choose cheaper alternatives, or buy in bulk to remain within budget.#

=== #5. Alerts#

#*Alerts* surface domain-level events that require *Household Member* attention. An *Alert*
may be triggered by a change in *Availability State* (item is now Low-Stock, Out-of-Stock,
Near-Expiry, or Expired) or by a *Budget* threshold crossing. A *Household Member* may
acknowledge an *Alert* by acting on it (triggering a *Replenishment Action*), deferring it
(snooze), or dismissing it (acknowledge without acting).#

#An *Alert* must not repeat without a corresponding change in domain state. Repeated
identical alerts without new triggering events constitute a domain invariant violation.#

=== #6. Shared Households and Coordination#

#In a *Household* with more than one *Household Member*, coordination across members is
required to prevent *Coordination Failures*. Members must share a consistent view of the
*Tracked Inventory Record*, agree on the scope of *Expenditures* (personal vs. shared),
and communicate *Replenishment Actions* to avoid duplicates.#

#The informal coordination mechanisms currently in use — group chat messages, whiteboard
lists, verbal agreements — are unreliable and the primary cause of the coordination
failures this system is designed to address.#

=== #7. Membership and Change#

#*Household* membership changes over time: members may join, leave, or change roles. When
membership changes, the *Tracked Inventory Record* and *Expenditure* history must remain
consistent and attributable. Items marked as personal to a departing member must be
explicitly transferred, archived, or removed from shared visibility.#
