=== #Risk Identification using FMEA and Guidewords#

#This section identifies requirement-level risks using two structured techniques:
Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA) and Guideword analysis.#

#The purpose of this subsection is to identify realistic failure situations and edge cases
related to the home inventory system's core functionality.
These risks are requirement-level concerns that may affect reliability, usability, correctness, and trust.#

==== #FMEA Risk Case 1 — Incorrect Quantity After Transaction#

[cols="1,4",options="header"]
|===
|Field |Description

|Failure mode
|The quantity in a tracked inventory record becomes incorrect after a transaction is recorded.
|Cause
|A transaction is recorded with the wrong quantity, multiple household members update the same tracked inventory record close together, or a manual adjustment is entered incorrectly.
|Effect on the system
|Low-stock judgment may become incorrect, alert / notification records may be triggered unnecessarily or not triggered when needed, and the restock list / planner may contain incorrect item types.
|Severity
|8

|Occurrence
|5

|Detection
|5

|Risk Priority Number
|200

|Scenario
|One household member records replenishment while another records consumption for the same tracked inventory record.
The final recorded quantity does not reflect the actual household inventory, causing an incorrect low-stock judgment.
|===

==== #FMEA Risk Case 2 — Scheduled Sweep Does Not Run#

[cols="1,4",options="header"]
|===
|Field |Description

|Failure mode
|The scheduled sweep does not execute at the expected time.
|Cause
|Scheduler failure, missed cron execution, service downtime, or configuration errors in the automation process.
|Effect on the system
|Near-expiry and expired item instances are not evaluated on time, low-stock conditions may remain undetected, and alert / notification records may be delayed or not created.
|Severity
|9

|Occurrence
|3

|Detection
|4

|Risk Priority Number
|108

|Scenario
|The scheduled sweep is expected to run once per day, but an automation failure prevents the run from occurring.
Expired item instances remain undetected until a later run or manual review.
|===

==== #FMEA Risk Case 3 — Duplicate Alert / Notification Records#

[cols="1,4",options="header"]
|===
|Field |Description

|Failure mode
|The system creates duplicate alert / notification records for the same unchanged condition.
|Cause
|State transitions are not checked correctly, deduplication rules are incomplete, or retries reinsert the same alert / notification record.
|Effect on the system
|Household members may experience notification fatigue, ignore future alerts, or lose trust in the system's automation.
|Severity
|7

|Occurrence
|4

|Detection
|4

|Risk Priority Number
|112

|Scenario
|A tracked inventory record remains LOW_STOCK across several evaluations.
If the system creates a new alert / notification record each time without checking whether the state changed, duplicate records accumulate.
|===

==== #Guideword Case 1 — Missing#

#*Guideword:* Missing#

#*Scenario:*
A household member consumes or replenishes an item type but does not record the transaction.#

#*System impact:*
The tracked inventory record diverges from household inventory reality, low-stock judgment becomes less reliable, and later automation decisions may be incorrect.#

#*Possible mitigation:*
Support low-friction logging, allow quick manual adjustments, and use transaction history to correct tracked inventory records over time.#

==== #Guideword Case 2 — Delayed#

#*Guideword:* Delayed#

#*Scenario:*
The scheduled sweep runs later than expected, so near-expiry or expired item instances are detected after the intended time.#

#*System impact:*
Alert / notification records are generated late, household members may not act in time, and expired 
item instances may remain in use longer than expected.#

#*Possible mitigation:*
Record the last successful scheduled sweep run, detect missed runs, and execute a catch-up sweep when necessary.#

==== #Guideword Case 3 — Duplicate#

#*Guideword:* Duplicate#

#*Scenario:*
The same item type is repeatedly added to the restock list / planner even though it is already present.#

#*System impact:*
The restock list / planner becomes cluttered, household members may become confused, and the system may appear unreliable or overly aggressive.#

#*Possible mitigation:*
Require deduplication before auto-add, allow household members to remove item types manually, and re-add only when a new qualifying state transition occurs.#

==== #Relationship to Requirement Concepts#

#These risks are directly 
related to the requirement-level concepts already identified in this section:#

- #*Fast capture* relates to the risk of missing transactions.#
- #*Tracked record vs reality* relates to quantity drift and inaccurate tracked inventory records.#
- #*Scheduled sweep* relates to delayed or missing automation runs.#
- #*State transition* relates to duplicate alert / notification records.#
- #*Auto-add to restock list / planner* relates to duplicate restock entries and user-control concerns.#

#This risk analysis supports later design decisions, validation activities, and reliability considerations.#
