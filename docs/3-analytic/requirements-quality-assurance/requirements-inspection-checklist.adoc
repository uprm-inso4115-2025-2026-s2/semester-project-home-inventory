== #Requirements Inspection Checklist (Defect-Driven)#

#This checklist standardizes requirement reviews so the team can detect common defects early and record them consistently.#

=== #Purpose#

#Use this checklist to review requirement statements, identify defects, classify their severity, and assign follow-up actions.#

=== #How to Use#

#. Select the requirement section to review.
. Check each requirement against the defect categories below.
. Record findings in the review log.
. Classify each issue as Critical, Major, or Minor.
. Propose a concrete fix and assign an owner.#

=== #Severity Guide#

#* *Critical* — blocks implementation or testing, or may cause major incorrect behavior.
* *Major* — creates significant ambiguity, inconsistency, or traceability problems.
* *Minor* — reduces clarity or structure but does not block progress.#

=== #Defect-Driven Checklist#

==== #Omissions#
#* Is every requirement defined somewhere?
* Are expected inputs, outputs, and system responses covered?
* Are edge cases such as missing data, invalid values, or no response addressed?#

==== #Contradictions#
#* Does the requirement conflict with another requirement, goal, or constraint?
* Are terms used consistently across documents?#

==== #Inadequacies#
#* Does the requirement reflect a real stakeholder need?
* Is it valid for realistic student or roommate usage scenarios?#

==== #Ambiguities#
#* Can the statement be interpreted in more than one way?
* Are vague terms such as "fast", "easy", or "soon" replaced with measurable criteria?#

==== #Unmeasurability#
#* Does the requirement include fit criteria such as time, threshold, count, or accuracy?
* Is there a clear pass/fail condition?#

==== #Noise#
#* Is the statement relevant to system goals or constraints?
* Would removing it change expected system behavior or quality?#

==== #Overspecification#
#* Does the requirement force a design decision too early?
* Could the same goal be satisfied in more than one way?#

==== #Poor Structuring#
#* Is the requirement located in the correct section?
* Is it mixed with assumptions, domain properties, or implementation details?#

==== #Opacity and Modifiability#
#* Are dependencies between requirements explicit?
* Would changing this requirement force scattered edits across many documents?#

=== #Minimum Team Usage Rule#

#* Each team should apply this checklist at least once per sprint to active requirements.
* Findings should be linked to GitHub issues or PR comments for traceability.#
