== Scenarios

[abstract]
Scenarios describe realistic end-to-end situations in the domain and how users expect things to work.

=== Scenario S1 — Low-Stock Trigger After Consumption
* Persona/actor: Julian, The Health-Conscious Student (primary); Occupant (system actor).
* Context: Julian keeps a personal shelf of gluten-free meal-prep items separate from roommates. He depends on specific brands that require advance ordering and wants the app to anticipate shortages based on normal weekly usage.
* Trigger: Julian records that he consumed/removed the last units of a pantry item (example, gluten-free pasta) during meal prep, causing the item quantity to fall below its low-stock threshold.

.Steps
. Julian opens the app after meal prep and selects the item “Gluten-free pasta” from his Inventory.
. Julian records consumption by decreasing the item quantity (e.g., from 2 boxes to 0).
. The system detects the updated quantity is below the defined low-stock threshold and flags the item as low-stock.
. The system adds the item to the Restock Planner with an initial status of “Needs restock”.
. The system issues a low-stock alert to Julian (push notification/in-app notiifcation), including the item name.


.Expected outcome
* The Inventory reflects the updated quantity and low-stock status for the item.
* The item appears in the Restock Planner as needing replenishment.
* Julian receives a low-stock alert in time to reorder before running out mid-week.

.Related needs / stories
* Needs: N3, N4, N9
* User stories: 
** US-ALT-01 (Recieve low-stock warnings)
** US-INV-01 (Add Items to inventory)
=== Scenario S2 — Shared Inventory Prevents Duplicate Purchase
* Persona/actor: Sofia, The Budget-Conscious Roommate (primary); Andrea, The Unofficial Household Manager (secondary); Occupant (system actor)
* Context: Sofia and her roommates share household essentials. Past issues include duplicate purchases due to out-of-date whiteboards and unclear ownership of shared items.
* Trigger: Sofia is about to purchase a shared essential (e.g., toilet paper) and checks the shared Inventory before paying.

.Steps
. Sofia opens the shared Inventory at the supermarket and searches for “toilet paper.”
. The system displays the current shared quantity and stock status (e.g., “In stock: 24 rolls”).
. Sofia sees the item is sufficiently stocked and decides not to buy it.
. Andrea later records a purchase for dish soap; the shared Inventory updates in real time for all Occupants.

.Expected outcome
* Sofia avoids buying a duplicate shared item, staying within budget.
* The shared Inventory remains accurate and synchronized across all Occupants.

.Related needs / stories
* Needs: N2, N5, N8, N10
* User stories: 
** US-INV-02 (See what belongs to whom)
** US-SB-01 (Split shared purchase with roomates)
** US-RPT-01 (See household dashboard with insights)
