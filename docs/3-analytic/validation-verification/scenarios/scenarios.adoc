== Scenarios

Scenarios describe realistic end-to-end situations in the domain and how stakeholders expect things to work.

=== Scenario S1a — Low-Stock Trigger After Partial Consumption
* Persona/actor: Julian, the health-conscious student; household member.
* Context: Julian keeps a personal shelf of meal-prep items separate from roommates. He depends on Barilla gluten-free elbows, which are not available locally and require online ordering with 3-day shipping. His low-stock threshold for this item is set to 2 boxes.
* Trigger: Julian records consumption after Sunday meal prep, reducing his tracked quantity from 3 boxes to 1 box—below his low-stock threshold of 2.
* Expected state transition: NORMAL → LOW_STOCK

.Steps
. Julian opens the system after Sunday meal prep and selects “Barilla gluten-free elbows” from his personal inventory shelf.
. In the app Julian sees that 3 boxes had remained before cooking this dinner. He used 2 boxes for the week's meal prep and decreases the count to 1.
. The system compares the new count to the low-stock threshold and flags Barilla gluten-free elbows as low-stock.
. The system adds “Barilla gluten-free elbows” to the restock list/planner with status “Needs restock” and reason “LOW_STOCK”.
. The system records and presents a low-stock alert to Julian: “Barilla gluten-free elbows is low — 1 box remaining.”

.Expected outcome
* The inventory shows “Barilla gluten-free elbows: 1 box” with a LOW_STOCK flag.
* The item appears in the restock list/planner as needing replenishment.
* Julian has enough lead time to reorder before running out mid-week.

=== Scenario S1b — Low-Stock and Out-of-Stock Simultaneously
* Persona/actor: Julian, the health-conscious student; household member.
* Context: Same as S1a, but Julian had only 2 boxes of Barilla gluten-free elbows remaining before preparing dinner. His low-stock threshold is set to 2 boxes.
* Trigger: Julian uses both remaining boxes for meal prep and decreases the count from 2 to 0.
* Expected state transition: NORMAL → OUT_OF_STOCK

.Steps
. Julian opens the system after meal prep and selects “Barilla gluten-free elbows.”
. He had 2 boxes remaining. He used both and decreases the count to 0.
. The system detects that the new count is both below the low-stock threshold and at zero. The system flags the item as OUT_OF_STOCK.
. The system creates a single combined alert: “Barilla gluten-free elbows is out of stock.” The out-of-stock condition takes priority over low-stock to avoid redundant alerts.
. The restock list/planner entry is created with reason “OUT_OF_STOCK.”

.Expected outcome
* Julian receives one alert, not two separate alerts.
* The restock list/planner shows the item with the most urgent reason.
* Julian's entire meal plan for the week is at risk; the alert conveys urgency.

=== Scenario S2 — Shared Inventory Prevents Duplicate Purchase
* Persona/actor: Sofia, the budget-conscious roommate; Andrea, the unofficial household manager; household members.
* Context: Sofia and her roommates share household essentials. Past issues include duplicate purchases due to out-of-date whiteboards and unclear responsibility for shared items. Sofia has a strict $50 weekly grocery budget.
* Trigger: Sofia is at the supermarket and is about to buy Charmin Ultra Soft toilet paper. She checks the shared inventory before paying.
* Expected state transition: No state change for “Charmin Ultra Soft toilet paper”; shared visibility converges after synchronization

.Steps
. Sofia opens the shared inventory on her phone at the supermarket and searches for “toilet paper.”
. The system displays: “Charmin Ultra Soft toilet paper — In stock: 24 rolls.”
. Sofia sees the item is sufficiently stocked and puts the 12-pack back on the shelf, saving $8.99 of her $50 weekly budget.
. Later that afternoon, Andrea records a purchase of Dawn dish soap at the wholesale club. The shared inventory updates for all household members once the change is recorded and synchronized.

.Expected outcome
* Sofia avoids buying duplicate toilet paper, keeping $8.99 within her budget.
* All household members see the updated shared inventory after synchronization.
