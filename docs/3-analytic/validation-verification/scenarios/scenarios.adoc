== Scenarios

Scenarios describe realistic end-to-end situations in the domain and how stakeholders expect things to work.

[NOTE]
====
Expiration-related scenarios and tests refer to the expiration state vocabulary defined in `alert-system-states.adoc` (Active, Expiring, Expired, Renewed, Deleted) and its example alert intervals (e.g., 7/3/1 days). Delivery infrastructure details (summarized in `tech-expiration-alert-system.adoc`) are treated as implementation considerations and are not assumed in scenarios unless explicitly stated.
====

=== Scenario S1 — Low-Stock Trigger After Consumption
* Persona/actor: Julian, the health-conscious student (primary); household member.
* Context: Julian keeps a personal shelf of meal-prep items separate from roommates. He depends on specific brands that require advance ordering and wants to avoid unexpected shortages during the week.
* Trigger: Julian records that he consumed the last units of a pantry item (e.g., gluten-free pasta), causing the tracked quantity to fall below its low-stock threshold.
* Expected state transition: NORMAL → LOW_STOCK (and Restock list/planner entry created)

.Steps
. Julian opens the system after meal prep and selects the item “Gluten-free pasta” from his inventory.
. Julian records consumption by decreasing the item quantity (e.g., from 2 boxes to 0).
. The system detects that the updated quantity is below the defined low-stock threshold and flags the item as low-stock.
. The system adds the item to the restock list/planner with an initial status of “Needs restock”.
. The system records and presents a low-stock alert to Julian (delivery method TBD), including the item name.

.Expected outcome
* The inventory reflects the updated quantity and low-stock status for the item.
* The item appears in the restock list/planner as needing replenishment.
* Julian becomes aware of the low-stock state in time to reorder before running out mid-week.

.Related needs / stories
* Needs: N1, N2
* User stories:
** US-ALT-01 (Receive low-stock warnings)
** US-INV-01 (Add items to inventory)


=== Scenario S2 — Shared Inventory Prevents Duplicate Purchase
* Persona/actor: Sofia, the budget-conscious roommate (primary); Andrea, the unofficial household manager (secondary); household members.
* Context: Sofia and her roommates share household essentials. Past issues include duplicate purchases due to out-of-date whiteboards and unclear responsibility for shared items.
* Trigger: Sofia is about to purchase a shared essential (e.g., toilet paper) and checks the shared inventory before paying.
* Expected state transition: No stock-state change for “toilet paper” (remains IN_STOCK); shared visibility converges after synchronization

.Steps
. Sofia opens the shared inventory at the supermarket and searches for “toilet paper.”
. The system displays the current shared quantity and stock status (e.g., “In stock: 24 rolls”).
. Sofia sees the item is sufficiently stocked and decides not to buy it.
. Later, Andrea records a purchase for dish soap; the shared inventory updates for household members once the change is recorded and synchronized.

.Expected outcome
* Sofia avoids buying a duplicate shared item, staying within budget.
* Household members converge on the same understanding of the shared inventory after changes are recorded.

.Related needs / stories
* Needs: N3, N4, N5
* User stories:
** US-INV-02 (See what belongs to whom)
** US-SB-01 (Split shared purchase with roommates)
** US-RPT-01 (See household dashboard with insights)
