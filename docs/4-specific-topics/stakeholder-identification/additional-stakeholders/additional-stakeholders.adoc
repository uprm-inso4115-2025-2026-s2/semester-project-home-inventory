== Additional Stakeholders

Stakeholders discovered later as the team learns more about the domain.

[NOTE]
====
*Lecture topic task summary (Non-Obvious Stakeholders):* As part of a lecture topic task on stakeholder identification, we expanded stakeholder analysis beyond the direct users (students living alone or with roommates) to include non-obvious stakeholders whose perspectives affect the domain and requirements. The task identified: (1) *Former roommates* (orphaned items, ownership transfer, move-out access removal), (2) *Future roommates* (pre-move-in planning and duplicate-avoidance via shareable inventory snapshots), (3) *Guests/visitors* (untracked consumption causing “shrinkage” and the need for reconciliation mechanisms), (4) *Parents/family contributors* (bulk/care-package contributions, visibility into needs without full access), (5) *Landlords/property managers* (landlord-owned vs tenant-owned items, restricted items, appliance maintenance), and (6) *Roommates assigned to live together who are strangers* (pre-household formation coordination and fair contribution tracking).

Across these stakeholders, three recurring phenomena emerged: *item ownership* (including transfer/relinquish/claim workflows), *inventory accuracy under external consumption*, and *notification timing* (alerts useful for contributors and coordination, but risky for noise). These findings motivate a flexible ownership model, multi-phase access (planning vs active household vs post-occupancy), limited external contributor views, and explicit handling of discrepancies/shrinkage and membership changes.
====

|Stakeholder |Goals / needs |Influence |Concerns / notes

|Landlords / Property Managers
|Ensure that tenant households are kept in good order and that shared spaces are not overloaded or misused.
|Low
|Unclear lines between personal and shared household property; no formal mechanism for landlord oversight.

|Grocery / Retail Stores
|Attract repeat purchases by students and household groups; provide accurate product data (barcodes, pricing) that inventory systems may rely on.
|Low
|Incorrect or inconsistent product metadata in retail databases causing failed barcode lookups.

|Mobile Platform Reviewers (App Store / Google Play)
|Ensure the mobile application complies with platform guidelines regarding user privacy, data handling, and interface standards before public distribution.
|High
|App rejection due to improper handling of user data or failure to meet minimum functionality requirements for listing.
