= Stakeholder Identification and Domain Description Units

== Stakeholder Identification

The Digital Home Inventory domain involves individuals and social structures that interact with physical household goods under financial and time constraints.

=== Primary Stakeholders

[upperalpha]
. Student Living Independently
+
Role:: Primary actor responsible for acquiring, storing, and replacing household items.
Goals:: Maintain household supplies, avoid running out of essential items, staying within budget.
Risks:: Purchasing duplicates, allowing items to expire, budget strain due to overspending.
Potential Conflicts:: Limited time vs need for organization, Budget constraints vs desire for convenience.

. Roommate
+
Role:: Co-resident who shares responsibility
Goals:: Fair sharing of responsibilities, avoiding duplicate purchases, maintain transparency.
Risks:: Lack of communication, unequal contribution to shared items, lack of coordination.
Potential Conflicts:: Disagreement over ownership of items, different consumption habits.

=== Secondary Stakeholders

[upperalpha]
. Parents/ Financial Support Providers
+
Role:: Indirect financial contributors to household expenses.
Goals:: Encourage responsible spending, ensure student maintains necessary essentials.
Risks:: Financial waste due to poor inventory awareness.

== Domain Description Units

=== Unit DH-01

Stakeholder:: Student
Category:: Inventory
Kind:: Entity
Attribute:: Static
Facet:: Insintric
Phenomena Names:: household item, quantity
Formal Statement:: A household item has a finite quantity that decreases through consumption and increases through acquisition.

=== Unit DH-02

Stakeholder:: Student
Category:: Inventory
Kind:: Event
Attribute:: Dynamic
Facet:: Intrinsic
Phenomena Names:: acquisition, purchase
Formal Statement:: Acquisition of a household item increases its available quantity and reduces available financial resources.
