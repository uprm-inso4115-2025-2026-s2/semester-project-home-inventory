== Initial Stakeholders

[abstract]
Initial set of stakeholders identified at the start of the project.

[cols="2,3,2,3",options="header"]
|===
|Stakeholder |Goals / needs |Influence |Concerns / notes

|Student living alone 
|Keep track of personal groceries, avoid food waste, and manage a tight budget efficiently. 
|High 
|Forgetting what items are currently in stock, letting perishable food expire before consumption.

|Student living with roommates 
|Coordinate shared groceries, maintain visibility of common household items, and avoid buying duplicates. 
|High 
|Lack of communication leading to excess inventory and wasted space in shared areas.

|Roommate who shares purchases 
|Track who bought what and when, ensuring equitable splitting of common goods like toilet paper, cleaning supplies. 
|High 
|Paying more than their fair share, or unexpectedly running out of essential shared items.

|Budget-conscious student
|Track consumption rates to predict expenses and minimize spending on unnecessary or duplicate items.
|Medium
|Overspending due to poor inventory visibility and unclear tracking of shared household purchases.

|Student with dietary/medical needs 
|Strictly monitor specific food items or medications, ensuring continuous availability of safe and necessary options. 
|Medium 
|Running out of essential specialized items; potential cross-contamination confusion in shared household tracking.

|Frequent household supply buyers 
|Fast data entry and easy management of bulk items to keep the inventory accurate without excessive effort. 
|Medium 
|Inventory entry taking too much time, making the application a burden rather than a helpful tool.

|Parents/Guardians 
|Ensure the student has enough food and supplies; potentially view shared shopping lists to help fund purchases. 
|Low 
|Money being wasted on expired food or duplicate purchases due to poor planning.

|Project managers / Course staff 
|Evaluate project execution, verify domain requirements are met, and assess the software engineering process. 
|High 
|The project failing to meet academic standards, syllabus requirements, or adequately solving the proposed domain problem.
|===

=== Stakeholder categories
* *Primary stakeholders:* Student living alone, Student living with roommates, Roommate who shares purchases.
* *Secondary stakeholders:* Budget-conscious student, Student with dietary/medical needs, Frequent household supply buyers, Parents/Guardians.
* *External stakeholders:* Project managers / Course staff.

=== Open questions
* How will users handle the transition of a "personal" item becoming a "shared" item if roommate agreements change?
* What is the threshold for a household supply to be considered "low stock" across different users' habits?
