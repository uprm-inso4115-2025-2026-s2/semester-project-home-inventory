== Stakeholder Identification

This section identifies stakeholders relevant to the domain and summarizes #their goals, influence, and concerns#.

=== #1. Primary Stakeholders (Direct Users)#

#These stakeholders interact with the system daily and their needs dictate the core functional requirements. They map directly to our defined user personas.#

==== #The Unofficial Household Manager (Persona: Andrea)#
* #**Who:** A frequent household supply buyer who naturally organizes the house and does bulk shopping.#
* #**Why:** Needs to keep the house perfectly stocked without carrying the entire mental load and wants to get reimbursed quickly for shared purchases.#
* #**How (Relationship):** Heavy, write-intensive usage. Creates shared inventories, logs bulk purchases, and sends reimbursement nudges.#
* #**Functional Needs:** Shared digital ledger, rapid data entry for bulk items, verifiable records of consumption, and low-stock alerts.#

==== #The Budget-Conscious Roommate (Persona: Sofia)#
* #**Who:** A student living in a shared apartment on a strict financial budget.#
* #**Why:** Needs to track shared groceries, split costs fairly, and completely avoid buying duplicate items due to limited funds and storage space.#
* #**How (Relationship):** Read/Write usage. Checks the app while at the store to verify current stock and logs personal vs. shared expenses.#
* #**Functional Needs:** Real-time inventory synchronization, duplicate purchase warnings, and distinct tracking of personal vs. shared household items.#

==== #The Busy Solo Student (Persona: Mateo)#
* #**Who:** A student living alone with severe time constraints and limited mental bandwidth for chores.#
* #**Why:** Needs to minimize time spent grocery shopping and avoid throwing away expired perishable food.#
* #**How (Relationship):** Notification-driven usage. Relies on the app to remind him of what he has before it goes bad.#
* #**Functional Needs:** Automated expiration alerts, simple UI for quick checks, and grocery list auto-generation based on low stock.#

==== #The Health-Conscious Student (Persona: Julian)#
* #**Who:** A student with strict dietary or medical needs who keeps their inventory separate from roommates.#
* #**Why:** Needs to maintain an uninterrupted supply of specialty items and track consumption rates of expensive supplements.#
* #**How (Relationship):** Precision usage. Sets exact thresholds for reordering.#
* #**Functional Needs:** Adjustable low-stock threshold alerts, strict permission/privacy boundaries for personal items within a shared household.#

=== #2. Secondary & Indirect Stakeholders#

#These individuals affect or are affected by the system but use it sporadically or indirectly.#

==== #Transient Household Members (Guests, Former/Future Roommates)#
* #**Who:** People temporarily interacting with the household inventory.#
* #**Why:** Guests consume items without tracking ("shrinkage"); former roommates leave orphaned items; future roommates need to see current stock before moving in.#
* #**Relationship:** Cause inventory drift or require access modifications.#
* #**Functional Needs:** Ownership transfer protocols, easy user removal/invitation, and inventory reconciliation mechanisms for untracked consumption.#

==== #Parents / Family Contributors#
* #**Who:** Family members helping fund the student's living expenses.#
* #**Why:** Want to ensure the student has enough food and isn't wasting money on duplicates or expired goods.#
* #**Relationship:** Read-only or indirect financial contributors.#
* #**Functional Needs:** Shareable grocery lists or read-only access to household needs.#

==== #Grocery / Retail Stores#
* #**Who:** Supermarkets and wholesale clubs.#
* #**Why:** Provide the products and barcode data the system relies on.#
* #**Relationship:** Data originators.#
* #**Functional Needs:** Accurate barcode scanning API integrations and manual entry fallbacks for unrecognized store brands.#

=== #3. External & Technical Stakeholders#

==== #Development Team & Database Maintainers (INSO4115 Team)#
* #**Who:** The software engineering students building and maintaining the app.#
* #**Why:** Need clear requirements, reliable backend uptime (Supabase), and clean architecture.#
* #**Functional Needs:** Well-documented APIs, robust database schema, automated backups, and CI/CD pipelines.#

==== #Project Evaluators (Project Managers / Course Staff)#
* #**Who:** Course instructors evaluating the software engineering process.#
* #**Why:** Assess the project against syllabus requirements and domain problem resolution.#
* #**Functional Needs:** End-to-end traceability, comprehensive AsciiDoc documentation, and functional completeness.#

==== #Mobile Platform Reviewers (App Store / Google Play)#
* #**Who:** Automated and manual reviewers for app stores.#
* #**Why:** Ensure the app complies with privacy, data handling, and UI guidelines.#
* #**Functional Needs:** Secure authentication, clear privacy policies, and crash-free navigation.#
