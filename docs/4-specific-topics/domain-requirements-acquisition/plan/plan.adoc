== Requirements Acquisition Plan

Plan for how we will gather and validate requirements from stakeholders and domain evidence. For Milestone 1, this plan is intentionally lightweight and focuses on collecting concrete examples that can be traced to needs, scenarios, and requirements.

=== Goals (Milestone 1)
* Capture real (“messy”) household practices instead of idealized descriptions.
* Understand shared-household coordination (visibility, duplicates, ownership/personal vs shared, and cost splitting).
* Identify the main friction points that cause tracking methods to be abandoned.
* Clarify key domain edge cases (bulk purchases, multiple locations, missing/unclear expiration dates).

=== Methods (Milestone 1)
* *Interviews (4–6):* Semi-structured interviews with solo students and students in shared households (including at least one student athlete). Focus on recent, specific incidents (waste, duplicates, shortages, roommate friction).
* *Contextual walkthroughs:* Ask participants to show their current artifacts (fridge notes/whiteboards/messages) and explain their shorthand and terminology.
* *Light competitive scan:* Review a small set of existing inventory/list apps to identify recurring friction patterns (especially entry effort and notification fatigue).

[NOTE]
====
Surveys and broader quantitative validation are considered useful but may be deferred if time is limited; Milestone 1 prioritizes rich qualitative evidence suitable for domain description and initial requirements.
====

=== Risks and mitigations (Milestone 1)
* *Risk:* Participants are too busy for long interviews.
** *Mitigation:* Keep sessions short (15–25 minutes) and allow asynchronous voice-note follow-ups.

* *Risk:* Participants describe ideal behavior rather than actual behavior.
** *Mitigation:* Ask for recent concrete examples (“last time you threw out food”, “last duplicate purchase”, “show your last note/chat”).

* *Risk:* Manual entry tolerance is near zero, making tracking unsustainable.
** *Mitigation:* Explicitly measure “effort tolerance” (seconds/taps) and capture what minimum information participants are willing to record.
