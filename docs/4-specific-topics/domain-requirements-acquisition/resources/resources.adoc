== Resources

Evidence sources and supporting materials used to understand the domain and justify requirements.

=== External sources

* **NAPO — National Association of Productivity & Organizing Professionals** +
  Provides standardized categorization principles for household organization. Informs the goal of organizing items by room or category to reduce search time and cognitive load during inventory management.

* **Insurance Information Institute (I.I.I.)** +
  Sets standards for proof of ownership in home inventory contexts. Informs the manual entry fields required for non-consumable items and the goal of tracking spending.

* **NBER — National Bureau of Economic Research (Household Finance)** +
  Validates the importance of budget-tracking as a behavioral commitment device for students. Supports the domain goal of spend visibility and the tight budget constraints of the primary stakeholder group.

* **Personal Information Management (PIM) research** +
  Key finding: users abandon tracking systems when real-time synchronization is required. Informs the design preference for event-driven or batch-entry models over continuous micro-updates.

* **Household Food Waste studies** +
  Key finding: students lack situational visibility of what they own, causing over-purchasing and waste. Directly supports the need for a digital inventory mirror and expiration alerting.

* **Endowment Effect in Asset Management** +
  Key finding: manual interaction with an item's cost increases conscious awareness of waste. Supports the use of manual entry as a commitment device for budget management.
